I am a final-year Industrial & Production Engineering student at BUET whose research is united by a single conviction: that careful statistical and machine learning analysis of real-world data — from World Bank development indicators to machining sensor readings — can generate insights that genuinely change decisions, policies, and systems.

With three peer-reviewed journal publications and five conference contributions across domains spanning public health analytics, environmental forecasting, manufacturing quality, and evolutionary game theory, I have built a research identity that is at once methodologically diverse and thematically coherent. I am actively seeking PhD positions in industrial engineering systems, operations research, and socio-technical decision modelling.

Research Projects

A Multi-Stakeholder Game-Theoretic Framework for Strategic Modeling of Digital Advertising Ecosystem

Ongoing

Developing a tripartite evolutionary game model to study incentive design and the funding of misinformation in digital advertising. The framework employs replicator dynamics, agent-based simulations, and sensitivity analysis to map strategic behaviour across advertisers, platforms, and content publishers. Targeting top-tier journals in operations research and information systems.

Evolutionary Game Theory Replicator Dynamics Multi-Agent Simulation Misinformation Modelling Incentive Design

Supervisors: Dr. Jun Zhuang (ISE, University at Buffalo) · Dr. Puneet Agarwal (IME, Cal Poly) · Dr. Ridwan Al Aziz (IPE, BUET)

GDP Growth and Infant Mortality: 20-Year Panel Analysis Across 30 Countries

Published

Examined the relationship between economic growth (log GDP) and under-5 infant mortality rates using World Bank data (2001–2020). Applied PLS/OLS regression with bootstrapping and ML feature importance, disaggregating results for high- and low-income nations to understand heterogeneous development effects. Published in AJIRI (2025).

Panel Regression World Bank Data PLS / OLS Public Health SHAP

Supervisor: Dr. Ridwan Al Aziz (IPE, BUET)

Emission Trajectories & Trade Patterns: K-Means Clustering + ARIMA Forecasting

Published

Grouped 10 developed countries by emission similarity using K-means clustering, then applied ARIMA modelling to project per capita carbon emissions and trade patterns over the next decade. The combined approach revealed divergent trajectories between industrialised and energy-transition economies. Published in JSERE (2025).

K-Means Clustering ARIMA Carbon Emissions Trade Forecasting Environmental Analytics

A Hierarchical Multi-Agent RL Approach to Recover Evolutionarily Stable Strategies in Large-Scale Evolutionary Games

Ongoing

Proposes a hierarchical MARL framework to address large-scale evolutionary games by identifying parameter configurations that induce convergence toward an Evolutionarily Stable Strategy (ESS). Classical analytical procedures become computationally intractable at scale; this approach uses PPO-based agents to recover ESS wherever one exists.

Evolutionary Game Theory Multi-Agent RL PPO ESS

Supervisor: Dr. Ridwan Al Aziz (IPE, BUET)
